Food Insecurity

California Mobility Center wants to transport excess food from hotels, restaurants, and other places with excess food to food pantries for those who need it while also keeping the food safe and secure. This project is focused on creating an internal tool called the Food Distribution Network, which creates a report illustrating a round-trip travel route for pick-up and drop-off locations of already-made foods to distribution points. The tool tracks the type of food (perishable, perishable-refrigerated, non-perishable), estimated spoil date, quantity (volume and weight), dietary details (nuts, dairy, etc), the time it was made, and estimated market value. The tool pulls from static data provided internally by our client. Our tool will calculate the best route based on the time the food was made and be displayed using Google Maps API. The current tool is prioritized for internal use but has the possibility for expansion and improvement . The main use of the tool is that it will help reduce the amount of time taken by California Mobility System professionals to create travel plans for pick-up and delivery.

Meet the Team

Food insecurity is an economic and social indicator of the health of a community. The U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) defines food insecurity as limited or uncertain availability of nutritionally adequate foods or uncertain ability to acquire these foods in socially acceptable ways. Poverty and unemployment are frequently predictors of food insecurity in the United States.
